A . P . J. ABDUL KALAM

1959-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, widely known as the "Missile Man of India, " was an inspiring figure whose contributions to science, education, and society have left an indelible mark. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become a prominent scientist and, eventually, the 11th President of India. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's defense and space programs, particularly in developing ballistic missile technology and launching India's satellite programs. His work on the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 solidified India's place as a nuclear power.
